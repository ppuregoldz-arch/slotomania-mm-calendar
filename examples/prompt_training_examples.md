# SM Prompt Training Examples

This document provides examples of effective and ineffective prompts for SM data analysis.

---

## Effective Prompts

### Example 1: Revenue Analysis
**Prompt:**
"Analyze daily Net revenue by CZ deluxe segment (0-5, 5-10, 10-20, 20-40, 40-60, 60-80, 80-100, +100) for the last 30 days to identify monetization opportunities and inform pricing strategy decisions."

**Quality Assessment:**
- **Clarity**: 5/5 - Clear and specific
- **Scope**: 5/5 - Metrics, segments, and timeframe defined
- **Complexity**: 5/5 - Appropriate for business needs
- **Actionability**: 5/5 - Clear business objective

**Why It's Effective:**
- Specific metric (daily Net revenue)
- Defined segments (CZ deluxe segments)
- Clear timeframe (last 30 days)
- Business objective stated (monetization opportunities, pricing strategy)

---

### Example 2: User Engagement
**Prompt:**
"Compare spin frequency and session duration between paying and non-paying users for Q1 2025 to optimize engagement strategies and identify conversion opportunities."

**Quality Assessment:**
- **Clarity**: 5/5 - Clear comparison request
- **Scope**: 5/5 - Metrics and segments defined
- **Complexity**: 4/5 - Appropriate complexity
- **Actionability**: 5/5 - Clear business objective

**Why It's Effective:**
- Specific metrics (spin frequency, session duration)
- Defined segments (paying vs non-paying)
- Clear timeframe (Q1 2025)
- Business objective stated (engagement optimization, conversion)

---

## Ineffective Prompts

### Example 1: Too Vague
**Prompt:**
"Show me revenue data"

**Quality Assessment:**
- **Clarity**: 1/5 - Vague and ambiguous
- **Scope**: 1/5 - Missing key parameters
- **Complexity**: 1/5 - Too simple, unclear needs
- **Actionability**: 1/5 - No business objective

**Why It's Ineffective:**
- No specific metric
- No timeframe
- No segments
- No business objective

**Improved Prompt:**
"Analyze daily Net revenue by CZ deluxe segment for the last 30 days to identify monetization opportunities."

---

### Example 2: Missing Timeframe
**Prompt:**
"Compare user behavior by segment"

**Quality Assessment:**
- **Clarity**: 2/5 - Some clarity but missing details
- **Scope**: 2/5 - Missing timeframe
- **Complexity**: 2/5 - Unclear complexity needs
- **Actionability**: 2/5 - Limited business value

**Why It's Ineffective:**
- No specific metrics
- No timeframe
- Vague segments
- No business objective

**Improved Prompt:**
"Compare spin frequency and session duration between CZ deluxe segments 20-40 and 40-60 for Q1 2025 to optimize engagement strategies."

---

### Example 3: Currency Confusion
**Prompt:**
"Show total revenue including slotobucks"

**Quality Assessment:**
- **Clarity**: 2/5 - Misunderstands currency types
- **Scope**: 2/5 - Missing key parameters
- **Complexity**: 2/5 - Incorrect approach
- **Actionability**: 2/5 - Misleading business value

**Why It's Ineffective:**
- Attempts to mix currencies (incorrect)
- No timeframe
- No segments
- No business objective

**Improved Prompt:**
"Show real money revenue (USD) and Slotobucks redemption (virtual currency) separately by product group for the last month to analyze monetization and currency injection patterns."

## Prompt Improvement Process

### Step 1: Assess Current Prompt
Evaluate using rubric:
- Clarity (1-5)
- Scope (1-5)
- Complexity (1-5)
- Actionability (1-5)

### Step 2: Identify Improvement Areas
- Missing metrics
- Missing timeframe
- Missing segments
- Missing business objective
- Currency confusion
- Aggregation issues

### Step 3: Provide Improved Prompt
- Add specific metrics
- Define timeframe
- Specify segments
- State business objective
- Clarify currency separation
- Specify aggregation methodology

### Step 4: Document Learning
- Record original prompt
- Document improvements
- Track prompt quality evolution
- Share best practices

## SM-Specific Prompt Patterns

### Revenue Analysis Pattern
"Analyze [metric] by [segment] for [timeframe] to [business objective]"

**Example:**
"Analyze daily Net revenue by CZ deluxe segment for the last 30 days to identify monetization opportunities."

### User Engagement Pattern
"Compare [metrics] between [segments] for [timeframe] to [business objective]"

**Example:**
"Compare spin frequency and session duration between paying and non-paying users for Q1 2025 to optimize engagement strategies."

### Economy Analysis Pattern
"Analyze [economy metrics] by [segment] for [timeframe], including [distribution metrics], to [business objective]"

**Example:**
"Analyze coin consumption rates by CZ deluxe segment for the last 30 days, including balance distribution percentiles, to optimize economy balance."

### Product Performance Pattern
"Analyze [product metrics] by [product dimension] for [timeframe] to [business objective]"

**Example:**
"Analyze revenue by product group for the last month, including value-for-money metrics, to inform product development priorities."

## Common Issues and Solutions

### Issue 1: Missing Timeframe
**Problem**: "Analyze revenue by segment"
**Solution**: Add specific timeframe: "for the last 30 days"

### Issue 2: Currency Confusion
**Problem**: "Show total revenue including slotobucks"
**Solution**: Separate currencies: "Show real money revenue and Slotobucks redemption separately"

### Issue 3: Missing Segments
**Problem**: "Compare user behavior"
**Solution**: Specify segments: "between CZ deluxe segments 20-40 and 40-60"

### Issue 4: Missing Business Objective
**Problem**: "Show product data"
**Solution**: Add objective: "to inform product development priorities"

### Issue 5: Aggregation Issues
**Problem**: "Show average DAU for January"
**Solution**: Specify methodology: "using two-step aggregation methodology"

These examples demonstrate effective prompt patterns and common improvement opportunities for SM data analysis.
