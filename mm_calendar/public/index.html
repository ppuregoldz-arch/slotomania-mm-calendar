Forecast v11 — self-calibrating forecast + Jan+ product knowledge (short)
  • Nothing below is hand-typed. DOW baselines, month-level crowd/trend, every promo bonus, and event-density scaling are all recomputed from real data every time scripts/calibrate_model.py runs (part of the daily pipeline).
  • Forecast coefficients use the CV-winning windows: 105 days for revenue and 45 days for PU. The full Jan+ history is still used in the Revenue tab as a separate product/combo knowledge base, because broad history helps planning but can overfit daily forecasts.
  • Promo bonuses are fit jointly (multivariate ridge regression across all 18 promo types at once, not one at a time) so co-occurring promos can't double-credit each other.
  • Month-level "crowd" (user-base size) uses each month's own flat average once well-observed, but the current, mostly-future month uses a continuous day-by-day trend fit from the trailing real days instead of freezing at one anchor point.
  • Day-of-month curve is now fit directly from data (was a hand-fixed +25/+12/0/−10/0/−12 shape) — real data shows week 1 is actually below the DOW baseline and month-end dips much deeper than previously assumed.
  • Promo-time carryover: a fraction of yesterday's promo bonus bleeds into today (13:00 UTC plan switch) — plus a small post-sale absorption penalty the day after a standalone Coin Sale.
  • Event bonus scales with promo-cluster density, not a flat amount (Cinco de Mayo ≠ Father's Day) — the density coefficients themselves are fit from real event days too.
  • Check the header's 🔄 Live-calibrated chip for current sample size and cross-validated accuracy (held-out days, not the days used to fit) — it updates every time the pipeline runs.

🎯 Recommendations — Core (coin sink)

What the data actually supports

Sun sees the largest PU coin-balance growth (+9%) and Mon the smallest (-10%) — a day-of-week pattern, not a sink-mechanic-driven one. If coin-sink pressure is a planning goal, weight it by day of week rather than by which sink mechanic is scheduled.

What the data does NOT support (yet)

Cross-validation shows no reliable per-mechanic effect on PU coin balance beyond the DOW baseline with the current ~93-day sample — resist the temptation to pick a "best" sink mechanic from this data; it would be overfitting noise. Revisit once more days accumulate.

🎯 The month has one real peak, not two

Days 1-2 (month-open) is the only period meaningfully above baseline (+38K). Every other period of the month runs flat-to-negative, including Month-end at -42K — the real trough. This contradicts the older assumption that week 1 is a second strong period: the data now shows it running below baseline once month-open is separated out. Planning implication: if a headline promo is being held back for "week 1 momentum," the data says that momentum isn't there — consider whether it would do more good reinforcing month-open (already the peak, compounding a strong start) or countering the month-end dip (where every $ has to fight a structural −42K headwind).

Day-of-week isn't just a revenue pattern

Sat/Fri are the strongest revenue days ($686K/$659K); Tue/Thu the weakest ($578K/$580K). Monday has by far the most payers (33,556 PU) but the lowest PU coin-balance growth (-10% vs +5% on Friday) — consistent with Monday's payer mix skewing toward smaller/newer converters (Dash Day breadth), not depth. Don't schedule your biggest single-ticket offer expecting Monday's ARPU to match Saturday's.

Trend, not just level

Base is moving, not flat: the trailing-real-days trend for 2026-07 shows revenue rising ~$0.46K/day and PU rising (recent short-term signal) ~41/day. These are short (~21-day) extrapolations, not the full documented multi-month decline — treat the PU uptick cautiously; it can reverse. Either way, flat monthly targets understate the shape of the month — plan day-by-day, not off a single monthly average.

⚖️ Can PU / Revenue grow without hurting the economy?
Important correction: revenue/PU-driving offers (Coin Sale, MGAP, Rolling, Buy All — purchase/injection mechanics) and coin/gem-sink mechanics (PYP, Dash, Shiny Show — gameplay features that consume currency) are classified from largely different, non-overlapping promo vocabularies — see the Core/Gems tabs for why offers were the wrong regressor there. That means there usually isn't a direct "this exact promo's revenue lift vs. its own economy cost" comparison to make — they're different levers. Custom Pod is the one exception: it's structured as a coin-sink tool that's also sold as a purchase, so it's the one lever with a real dual-signal reading.

Revenue/PU side: what's actually driving growth

Top offers by revenue lift: Custom Pod (+29.8K), ix_customPod_x_happyHour (+19.9K), ix_customPod_x_coinSale (+15.0K), ix_coinSale_x_decoyBonanza (+15.0K). Weakest: Golden Spin (-30.1K), Buy All (-10.4K).

Economy side: what plausibly drains coins

Top coin-sink mechanics by (small, mostly-noise) coin-velocity delta: Win Master (+0.41pp, n=11), M.E.S Tokens/Steps (+0.26pp, n=58), Custom Pod (+0.19pp, n=12). Per the Core tab's cross-validation, none of these reliably beat day-of-week yet — treat as directional, not a lever to pull.

The one dual-purpose lever

Custom Pod is both the strongest revenue offer (+29.8K) and one of the few sink mechanics with a flat-to-negative coin-balance reading (+0.19pp) — consistent with how it's structured (a coin sink that's sold, not a coin-injection tool). That's the closest thing to a "grows revenue without inflating balances" signal in the data, though with the same small-sample caveat as everything else in the economy model.

The honest overall answer

Because revenue-driving offers and coin/gem-sink mechanics are largely separate scheduling decisions, growing revenue/PU via offer selection doesn't mechanically require changing which sink mechanics run — the two are planned somewhat independently already. Combined with the Core/Gems finding that sink-mechanic choice barely moves the PU balance metric beyond day-of-week anyway, the defensible conclusion is: there's no visible near-term economy cost to optimizing the offer mix for revenue/PU — not because a clean trade-off was found and avoided, but because the data doesn't show promo-level decisions on either side moving the economy metric much at all. Re-check this if payment or spend velocity ever shows real strain.

🧠 Key placement insights

Real depth vs conditional toppers

True standalone depth (survives holiday-clean): Custom Pod (+8.9%), Coin Sale (+4.2%) → peak days/Saturday. Extreme Stamp/MGAP Bigger looked strong but were holiday-confounded — conditional toppers (Revenue tab). Breadth (many payers, lower ARPPU): Price Cut → widen funnel, Mondays.

Economy

MGAP = largest coin sink (246–248M spins). Gem amplifiers (Rolling MoreForLess $51K, Coin Sale $48K, GGS $47K, Boosted Gemback $42K) drive gem purchases. Gem balance ~9–10K · Slotobucks ~385K.

Timing

Front-load Week 1 (strongest, but gap more modest than v1 — see v2), protect Week 3 (trough) with retention/value. Do not waste amplifiers on Tue/Thu. Sat→depth, Mon→breadth.

Momentum

Sale/Extreme days cause next-day drop of only a mild (~$8K) still above baseline → back-to-back amplifiers (Fri→Sat) are safe. ⚠️ part of this is holidays (Revenue tab).

📈 User trend (quarterly)
DAU slowly declining 424K → 406K (~4%/quarter), but revenue holds ($620–650K) via rising ARPU and stable engagement (sessions ~4.2). Monetization works harder on a shrinking base → support retention.
