# Weekly Stickiness Analysis: Album Launches Impact

**Analysis Date:** May 24, 2026  
**Scope:** Last 5 album launches  
**Metric:** User stickiness (average active days per week per active user)  

## Key Findings

### Overall Stickiness Levels
- **Baseline Engagement:** Users average **4.5-4.6 active days per week** during both periods
- **Consistent Behavior:** Stickiness remains remarkably stable around album launches
- **High Engagement:** The 4.5+ daily sessions per week indicates strong user commitment

### Album Launch Impact Analysis

| Album | Launch Date | Before Stickiness | After Stickiness | Change | WAU Change |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|------------------|---------|------------|
| Funky Family Album | May 12, 2026 | 4.588 | 4.572 | -0.016 | +665 users |
| Royal Album | Feb 24, 2026 | 4.573 | 4.589 | +0.016 | -11,423 users |
| The Winter Tales Album | Dec 9, 2025 | 4.503 | 4.497 | -0.006 | -3,017 users |
| Lucy's Album | Sep 16, 2025 | 4.440 | 4.499 | +0.059 | -14,781 users |
| The Sunny Days Album | Jul 15, 2025 | 4.502 | 4.487 | -0.015 | -11,685 users |

### Key Insights

#### 1. **Minimal Stickiness Impact**
- Album launches show **very small changes** in user stickiness (-0.020 to +0.060)
- No consistent pattern of increase or decrease post-launch
- Changes are within normal variance range

#### 2. **User Base Dynamics**
- **Recent albums** (2026) maintain or slightly increase WAU after launch
- **Older albums** (2025) show WAU decreases after launch, possibly due to:
  - Natural user base evolution over time
  - Seasonal effects
  - Market competition

#### 3. **Engagement Stability** 
- **High baseline stickiness** (4.4+ days/week) suggests already-engaged user base
- **Limited room for improvement** - users are already highly active
- **Album content** may maintain engagement rather than dramatically increase it

## Business Implications

### Content Strategy
- **Albums maintain engagement** rather than dramatically boost it
- Focus should be on **retention and monetization** of already-engaged users
- Consider complementary features to drive incremental engagement

### User Acquisition vs. Retention
- Current user base shows **excellent engagement levels**
- Album launches may be more effective for **retention** than **engagement frequency**
- WAU changes suggest albums impact **user acquisition/retention** more than **session frequency**

### Performance Benchmarking
- **4.5+ active days per week** represents strong engagement baseline
- Future album launches can be benchmarked against this stickiness range
- Focus metrics on **WAU growth** and **monetization** rather than frequency increases

## Recommendations

1. **Maintain Current Engagement Quality** - 4.5+ days/week is excellent
2. **Focus on WAU Growth** - Recent albums show positive user base expansion
3. **Monitor Long-term Trends** - Track whether stickiness degrades over album lifecycle
4. **Cross-Feature Analysis** - Investigate how albums interact with other features for engagement

## Methodology Notes

- **Period Definition:** Week before (-7 to -1 days) vs. week after (+1 to +7 days) album launch
- **User Exclusions:** Playtika employees and test users excluded
- **Data Source:** `agg.agg_sm_daily_users_stats` for reliable daily activity tracking
- **Validation:** Manual calculation verified for Funky Family Album periods
